Brazilian Cheese Bread

Description

The quickest, easiest, short-cut method of making Brazilian cheese bread is what I describe here (and the only way some of my Brazilian friends make it). You put the ingredients in a blender. Pour them out into a mini-muffin tin and bake.

The beauty of this recipe is that you can make a big batch of batter and just store it in the refrigerator (for up to a week), pouring out just as many mini-muffins as you want to eat. You can even cook them in a toaster oven.

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg, room temperature
  • 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil, plus more for greasing the pan
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 cups (170 g) tapioca flour
  • 1/2 cup (66 g) packed grated or crumbled cheese, your preference
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or more to taste)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400Â°F and prepare a mini muffin tin
  2. Blend the ingredients into a batter
  3. Pour the batter into the muffin tin
  4. Bake in the oven for 15-20 minutes until puffy and browned
  5. Let cool for several minutes
  6. Enjoy!
